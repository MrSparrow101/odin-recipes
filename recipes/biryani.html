Sindhi Chicken Biryani

Description

Experience the vibrant and aromatic flavors of Pakistani Chicken Biryani, a beloved classic that perfectly captures the essence of South Asian cuisine. This iconic dish combines succulent chicken marinated in a blend of yogurt and fragrant spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. Layered with partially cooked basmati rice infused with saffron, caramelized onions, and fresh herbs, every bite is a symphony of taste and texture. The slow-cooking process, often referred to as 'Dum,' ensures that the spices and flavors infuse deeply into the chicken and rice, creating an irresistible harmony of aromas and tastes.

Ingredients:

For The Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 kg plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt to taste

For The Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For Layering:

  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced and fried until golden brown
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter

Steps:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Combine chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the Rice: Rinse basmati rice until the water runs clear. Boil water with bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt. Add rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken: In a large pot, cook the marinated chicken over medium heat until the chicken is tender and the masala thickens.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half the cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle fried onions, saffron milk, coriander, and mint leaves. Add the remaining rice and repeat the toppings.
  5. Dum Cooking: Drizzle ghee over the top. Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Serve: Gently mix the layers before serving. Serve hot with raita and a fresh salad.
